Module Overview

In this module students will learn about:

  • Practicum experiences and expectations
  • Primary practicum projects
  • Timeline of experiences
  • Tracking logs of time and activity
  • Supervision meetings and supervisor evaluations
Module Objectives

By the end of this module students should be able to:

  • Describe practicum experiences and expectations
  • List primary practicum projects
  • Locate tracking logs
  • Complete the Practicum Placement Request Form
  • Outline the practicum experiences and expectations for a site supervisor
NASP Standards

The content and expectation in this module are aligned with the following NASP Standards:

  • School psychologists understand and utilize assessment methods for identifying strengths and needs.
  • School psychologists understand varied models and strategies of consultation and collaboration.
  • School psychologists understand the biological, cultural, and social influences on academic skills.
  • School psychologists understand the biological, cultural, developmental, and social influences on mental and behavioral health..
  • School psychologists understand systems’ structures, organization, and theory.
  • School psychologists understand principles and research related to social–emotional well-being, resilience, and risk factors in learning.
  • School psychologists understand principles and research related to family systems, strengths, needs, and cultures.
  • School psychologists have knowledge of, and inherent respect for, individual differences, abilities, disabilities, and other diverse characteristics.
  • School psychologists have knowledge of research design, statistics, measurement, and varied data collection and analysis techniques.
  • School psychologists have knowledge of the history and foundations of school psychology; multiple service models and methods; ethical, legal, and professional standards.
